The story

Built because I kept
losing my own lines.

A few years ago I started writing my first book — a quiet, sprawling thing about love, faith, and the people we don't get to keep. It took me three years.

The hardest part wasn't the writing. It was that the ideas kept arriving when I wasn't writing. On walks, in the shower, at 2am with the phone screen lighting up my face. I'd open Notes, type something half-cooked, and never see it again.

By year two, I had hundreds of fragments scattered across Notes, Keep, screenshots, voice memos, and an embarrassing number of receipts. Some of them were the best lines in the book. Some I never found again.

So I built NovaLog — a single place where every fragment has a home, can be tagged by theme, and marked the moment it lands in the manuscript. The app is yours now. I hope it keeps your lines safer than I kept mine.

FAQ

Questions you might
be holding.

Is it really free?
Yes — and it stays free during early access. If a paid tier comes later (more features, team sharing), the basics will always be free.
I'm not writing a book. Can I still use it?
Absolutely. NovaLog was born from a book, but the model — capture, tag, mark when used — fits anything: a screenplay, a thesis, a music album, a Substack queue, a research project. Use the language that fits you.
Who can see my ideas?
Only you. Every fragment is stored against your account, protected by row-level security on the database. Even the developer can't browse your notes.
Can I export my data?
Yes. In the app sidebar, click Export JSON — a full backup of your ideas, tags, and statuses in one file. Import works the same way.
Can I delete my account?
Yes, completely. Account → Danger zone → Delete my account. Your data is wiped permanently within seconds — no soft-delete, no shadow archive.
Is the code open source?
The frontend is public on GitHub. Read it, fork it, host your own copy.
